Oops! You Fell for a Phishing Test

Don’t worry, this was just a test—but it could have been a real threat.

What Happened?

You clicked on a suspicious link in an email that was designed to look legitimate. This is exactly how cybercriminals trick people into giving up sensitive information.

What You Should Have Noticed:

  • Unusual urgency: The email mentioned “Failure to act within 24 hours…” or asked you to change a password urgently.
  • Suspicious sender: The sender’s email address wasn’t quite right (e.g., misspelled or unverified domain).
  • Link destination: The link you clicked didn’t go to a company website. Hovering over it should have revealed the actual destination.

How to Avoid Future Phishing Attacks:

  • Always double-check the sender’s email address.
  • Hover over links before clicking to see where they actually go.
  • Be wary of emails that request sensitive information or urgent actions.
  • Delete any suspicious email or inform someone if unsure..

Next Steps

Now that you've experienced this simulation, you're better equipped to identify and report phishing attacks in the future. Stay vigilant, and if you're ever unsure, don't click the link!.
